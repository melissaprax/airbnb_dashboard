Using 2019 AirBnB data, we created a JavaScript dashboard to visualize and analyze the data. Use the drop down to populate the map, histogram and wordcloud to one of five New York City neighborhoods. The data we used is from Inside AirBnB. This data has a Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International License.

Airbnb listing prices in this dataset range from $10 per night to $10,000. While a histogram on a linear scale with an x-axis range of 0-500 captures the vast majority of the price distribution (97.5% of listing), it fails to captures upper extremes and their variance over neighborhood groups. The violin plot above, while not interactive, displays the full range of listing price data using a logarithmic y-axis scale. In ascending order, the neighborhood groups are arranged according to their median listing price, ranging from $65 in the Bronx to $150 in Manhattan. The relative price of listing by room type (Entire home/apartment, Private Room, Shared Room) is consistent, as predicted, across all neighborhood groups.
